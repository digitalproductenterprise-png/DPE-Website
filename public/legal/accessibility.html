Accessibility Statement

Last updated: May 2026

Partially Conformant

Digital Product Enterprise is committed to making our website accessible to the widest possible audience, including people with disabilities. We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standard.

1. Our Commitment

We believe that everyone should be able to access information and services online, regardless of disability, age, or the technology they use. We are continuously working to improve the accessibility of our website and digital products.

This statement applies to the website at digitalproductenterprise.com and its associated pages, including our digital product delivery pages.

2. How Accessible Our Website Is

We know some parts of this website are not fully accessible. Our current level of conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA is partial. This means we meet many accessibility requirements, but there are areas we are still working to improve.

What works well

  • All pages use descriptive page titles
  • Text and background colours meet WCAG AA contrast requirements in most areas
  • The website is navigable using a keyboard alone
  • Form fields include visible labels
  • The website is responsive and works on mobile devices
  • Links have descriptive text (not just "click here")
  • Headings are structured in logical order (H1, H2, H3)

Known limitations

  • Some images may not have full alternative text descriptions — we are reviewing these
  • Some third-party embedded content (such as payment forms) may not fully meet WCAG 2.1 AA — this is outside our direct control
  • PDF documents and downloadable digital products may not be fully optimised for screen readers — we are working to improve this
  • Video content, if added in future, may initially lack captions or audio descriptions

3. Technical Specifications

Our website is built using modern web technologies including HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. We aim to ensure compatibility with the following assistive technologies and browsers:

  • Screen readers: NVDA (Windows), JAWS (Windows), VoiceOver (macOS/iOS), TalkBack (Android)
  • Browsers: Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Apple Safari, Microsoft Edge (latest versions)
  • Zoom and magnification: up to 200% without loss of content or functionality
  • Keyboard-only navigation throughout the site

4. Disproportionate Burden

We have not currently identified any areas where making improvements would constitute a disproportionate burden. We review this assessment annually. If this changes, we will document the reasons clearly in an updated version of this statement.

5. Digital Products Accessibility

Our digital products (PDF guides, templates, and written materials) are created with readability in mind. We use high-contrast formatting, clear headings, and readable font sizes. We acknowledge that some PDF documents may not be fully tagged for screen reader use, and we are working to improve this in future versions of our products.

If you purchase a digital product and require it in an alternative format, please contact us and we will do our best to accommodate your needs.

6. Reporting Accessibility Issues

We welcome feedback on the accessibility of our website and products. If you experience any barriers that are not listed here, or if you cannot access any part of our website, please let us know. We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 working days.

Contact us by email: support@digitalproductenterprise.com

Please include:

  • The URL of the page you were trying to access
  • A description of the problem you encountered
  • The assistive technology or browser you are using

7. Enforcement

If you are not satisfied with our response to your accessibility concern, you may contact the Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) which is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) Accessibility Regulations 2018 in England, Scotland, and Wales. However, please note that as a private business, DPE is not subject to the Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations. We nonetheless commit to these standards voluntarily.

You may also contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS) if you believe you have been discriminated against under the Equality Act 2010: equalityadvisoryservice.com.

8. What We Are Doing to Improve

We have an ongoing accessibility improvement plan that includes:

  • Regular accessibility audits of new and updated pages
  • Adding alternative text to all images as they are updated
  • Improving PDF accessibility for new digital product releases
  • Reviewing third-party integrations for accessibility compliance
  • Training for anyone who creates content for our website

9. Preparation of This Statement

This accessibility statement was prepared in May 2026 based on a self-assessment of our website against the WCAG 2.1 Level AA standard. We review and update this statement annually, or when significant changes are made to our website.
