Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Digital Product Enterprise ("DPE", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data. We are committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who We Are

Digital Product Enterprise is a UK-based business operating as an AI consultancy and digital products provider. We provide AI implementation services to businesses and sell digital educational products and training materials.

Contact details:
Digital Product Enterprise
Email: support@digitalproductenterprise.com

2. What Data We Collect

Information you provide directly

  • Name and contact details (email address, phone number)
  • Business name and role
  • Billing and payment information (processed securely through third-party payment providers; we do not store card details)
  • Enquiry and message content submitted through our contact forms
  • Information shared during discovery calls, audits, or consultations

Information collected automatically

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Referring URLs and search terms
  • Device type and operating system
  • Cookie data (see our Cookie Policy for full details)

Information from third parties

  • Payment processor confirmations (e.g. Stripe)
  • Email marketing platform data (e.g. open rates, click-through rates)

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To deliver our services — communicating with you, delivering AI audits, implementing projects, and providing ongoing support
  • To fulfil digital product orders — processing payment, delivering purchased materials, and providing access to courses or downloads
  • To communicate with you — responding to enquiries, sending booking confirmations, and providing customer support
  • To send marketing communications — only where you have given consent or where we have a legitimate interest, and always with an easy opt-out
  • To improve our website and services — analysing usage patterns and feedback to enhance our offerings
  • To comply with legal obligations — including tax records, anti-fraud measures, and regulatory requirements

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract — processing necessary to fulfil a service or product order you have placed
  • Legitimate interests — improving our services, fraud prevention, and direct marketing to existing clients
  • Consent — for marketing emails to new contacts and for non-essential cookies
  • Legal obligation — where required by UK law, such as tax and financial record-keeping

5. How Long We Keep Your Data

  • Client and project records: 7 years (to comply with HMRC requirements)
  • Marketing contacts who opt out: Suppression list retained to honour opt-out preferences
  • Enquiry data where no contract is formed: 12 months
  • Website analytics: As per the retention settings of our analytics provider (typically 26 months)

6. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell your personal data. We may share it with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our business, including:

  • Payment processors (e.g. Stripe) — to process transactions securely
  • Email platforms (e.g. Brevo/Mailchimp) — to send transactional and marketing emails
  • Website hosting and analytics providers — to operate and improve our website
  • Cloud storage providers — to store files and communications securely
  • Professional advisers — accountants and legal advisers as required

All third-party processors are contractually bound to handle your data securely and only for the purpose specified. Where data is transferred outside the UK, we ensure adequate safeguards are in place.

7. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your data where there is no lawful reason to retain it
  • Right to restrict processing — to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
  • Right to withdraw consent — at any time where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at support@digitalproductenterprise.com. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

8. Marketing Communications

If you have purchased from us or enquired about our services, we may contact you with relevant information about our services. You can opt out at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any email or by contacting us directly. We will never send you unsolicited marketing without a lawful basis to do so.

9. Website Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. Our website uses SSL/TLS encryption. However, no internet transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Children's Privacy

Our services and products are intended for business owners and adults aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under the age of 18.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of our website or services after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. We recommend checking this page periodically.
